JUCIFER

About JUCIFER:

• Founded in 1993, Jucifer consists of the married couple of Gazelle Amber Valentine (guitar,
vocals) and Edgar Livengood (drums). They are known to have pioneered the two piece
sludge/doom metal band, the single-instrument wall of sound, and the combination of melodic
hooks with monolithic down-tuned guitars recently popularized by newer bands. Valentine was
featured in Guitar Player magazine twice as a result of her ground breaking style.

• Jucifer is literally always on tour — since 2001, the couple has lived in its RV and had no
permanent residence, playing hundreds of shows throughout North America and Europe over the
course of each year. During their existence the band has played and toured with everyone from
Mastodon and High On Fire to Melt-Banana, Eyehategod, Melvins, Lubricated Goat and Mike Watt.

• Jucifer has been compared to the Melvins, Jesus Lizard, Black Sabbath, Slayer, Morbid Angel,
Neurosis, Sunn O))), Lightning Bolt, Celtic Frost, My Bloody Valentine, and Portishead. Jucifer
describes its own sound as: “A peasant revolt etched in bone at 140 db.”

• Gazelle Amber Valentine typically performs live with a wall of amplification, at times using as many
as 25 cabinets but recently averaging 19; 5 guitar cabs and 14 bass cabs. She has said the band
brings a stadium show production to the club and theater setting.

• Slayer’s Dave Lombardo names Jucifer drummer Edgar Livengood as one of his two favorite
drummers, along with The Melvins’ Dale Crover.

• Jucifer has albums out on labels such as Capricorn, Alternative Tentacles, and Relapse.

• Jucifer’s previous album, 2008’s L’Autrichienne, was a concept album about Marie Antoinette and
the French Revolution.

• Jucifer is the subject of two films currently in the works — one with director David Hall (known for
his Maryland Deathfest documentary and his feature films based on the music of Today is the Day
and Fuck the Facts) and one with director Derek Cianfrance (director of Blue Valentine, starring
Ryan Gosling and Michelle Williams.)

• Jucifer’s seventh album, 2010’s Throned In Blood, is a concept album about the atrocities of war.
Throned In Blood was released on Jucifer’s own new label, Nomadic Fortress. It has been a
longtime goal of the band to start its own label; moving toward a fully self-sufficient model.
Relapse continues to support Jucifer and has inked a distribution deal with Nomadic Fortress to
distribute its output. Forthcoming releases by Nomadic Fortress will include other bands along
with Jucifer’s own material. Alternative Tentacles has licensed the LP version of Throned In Blood
as they did with L’Autrichienne.

Nomadic Fortress will reissue Jucifer’s 1994 4-track EP Nadir in summer 2011, and plans to issue a
split featuring Jucifer & Aaron Deal (Salome) in fall 2011. Jucifer is in the process of recording a
new studio album as well, slated for release in spring of 2012.

• Jucifer toured North America, Europe, and Russia throughout 2010 and 2011 in support of
Throned in Blood, including Maryland Deathfest 2010, SWR Barroselas 2010 (Portugal), Stoned Hand
Of Doom 2010 (Italy), Asymmetry 2011 (Poland) and Durbuy Rock 2011 (Belgium).
